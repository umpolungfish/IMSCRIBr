I added the canonical ouroboricity tier to proof_scaffold.py.

ouroboricity_tier(ops) builds the 12-primitive tuple the opcodes procedurally imscribe and
returns cl8nk_navigator.assess_tier on it — the Grammar's own verdict, not a first/last-token
proxy. emit_scaffold now prints this as the Expected tier and gates its (by decide) arms on
it. This is the single source of truth every ob3ect emitter calls (auto, batch_diagrams, the
scaffold wrapper), so the tier is always the Grammar's, never a guess and never my bias.

The honest verdict from this scorer is O₀ and O₁ only: the opcode-to-field map can drive just
three of the eight tier-scoring primitives — criticality, winding, recognition — to their
terminal values, so O₁ is the ceiling for anything built from the current opcodes. I let that
stand as the Grammar being self-diagnostic.
