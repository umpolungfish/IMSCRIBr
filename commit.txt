symbolic_diagram.py: reconstruct the black-and-white pen-mode renderer

The pen-mode wiring renderer documented in ob3ect/READING_GUIDE.md had been lost:
render_wiring_svg_v3 accepted pen_mode but ignored it, and the real renderer was
never committed (no commit, stash, reflog, or dangling blob has it). An earlier
attempt to fake it by grayscale-inverting the color renderer was wrong (that keeps
filled nodes, no line patterns, no hatch, no vertical legend) and has been reverted.

Reconstructed the true pen renderer from READING_GUIDE.md + the surviving sample
SVGs, driven by the same compute_layout engine as the color path:

  render_wiring_pen_svg(graph, name, ourobor, description):
    - white canvas (1220x630), black ink, faint gray grid
    - register-state hatch <pattern> defs: hatch-T (vertical), hatch-F (horizontal),
      hatch-B (cross); drawn as the node's inner register fill
    - unfilled family node shapes: circle=LOGICAL, diamond=FROBENIUS,
      hexagon=DIALETHEIA, square=LINEAR; 2-letter label above, full name below
    - 12 token-specific edge line patterns (solid / dash-dot-dot / bold / dashed /
      double / zigzag / crosshatch), midpoint glyphs (IMSCRIB back-arrow, FSPLIT
      diamond, FFUSE dot, EVALT +, EVALF x), open/filled/reverse arrowheads,
      nesting-depth thickness
    - guard ports: open circle (input, left), filled circle (output, right)
    - register-delta labels on edges; IFIX double-barrier with x markers;
      FSPLIT<->FFUSE pair brackets with circled numerals
    - vertical left legend: EDGES / GUARD / NODES / PAIRS / REG delta
    - footer ouroboricity

  render_wiring_svg_v3 gains pen_mode back in its signature and delegates to the
  pen renderer when pen_mode=True.

Verified: ob3ect auto.py _write_diagrams now emits demo_diagram.svg (color, 18
saturated colors) and demo_diagram_pen.svg (0 saturated = pure B&W, with hatch
defs and legend); rendered to PNG the pen diagram matches the documented pen
schema and the original sample style.
